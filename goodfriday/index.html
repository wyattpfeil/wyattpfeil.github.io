10 Reasons Tech-Focused Schools Should Have Off on Good Friday

A Guide for Superintendents

Why Good Friday Is Worth a Day Off for Tech-Focused Campuses

Welcome to Wyatt's Tips for Superintendents! In this article, we focus on why Good Friday should be recognized as a day off at technology-oriented institutions. These reasons reflect both the unique demands and inclusive culture of schools known for their innovation and diverse student body.

1. Respect for Religious & Cultural Diversity

A technology-focused campus typically attracts a global community of students and staff. Recognizing a significant religious holiday demonstrates respect for those who observe Good Friday, supporting an environment of cultural inclusion.

2. Support Mental Health & Academic Balance

High-level engineering, computing, and design programs can be rigorous. Granting a day off during the spring semester allows students and faculty to reset, rest, and return with renewed motivation.

3. Reduced Campus Services & Regional Norms

Many local businesses and services close on Good Friday. A day off helps align your institution’s schedule with community norms, ensuring students and staff aren’t inconvenienced by limited access to resources off-campus.

4. Minimizing Absenteeism & Class Disruptions

Students and staff who do observe Good Friday often plan travel or gatherings around it. Scheduling time off prevents excessive absences and keeps classes on track for those who rely on hands-on projects and labs.

5. Collaborative Projects & Lab Schedules

In tech-heavy curriculums, group projects and labs require coordinated attendance. A day off preempts attendance issues, allowing project teams to maintain consistent workflow before or after the holiday.

6. Opportunity for Alternative Engagement

Rather than hosting classes, the school can organize community service or professional development events on Good Friday for those who do not observe the holiday, fostering broader engagement without interrupting the regular academic schedule.

7. Campus Culture of Inclusion

Tech institutions thrive on collaboration across a wide range of backgrounds and beliefs. Offering Good Friday off underlines the institution’s commitment to celebrating diversity and ensuring all feel welcomed.

8. Potential Timing Before Major Exams

As spring semester intensifies, students often face major exams, presentations, or project deadlines. A short break can help relieve pressure and increase overall academic performance.

9. Supports Faculty & Staff Well-Being

Professors and staff in fast-paced tech environments shoulder a lot of responsibility—from mentoring students to conducting research. A day off can be invaluable for catching up on personal matters or recharging.

10. Strengthening Community Ties

Offering Good Friday off encourages students and employees to participate in local events, volunteer opportunities, or religious services—bridging the campus with the broader community and reinforcing the institution’s public commitment to diversity.
